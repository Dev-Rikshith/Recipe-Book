# Pachi Mirchi Kodi Pulao

*A fragrant Andhra-style chicken pulao where the rice and chicken are cooked together, flavoured with a bold green chilli, coriander, and curd marinade.*

---

## Ingredients

### Marinade
- 1 kg chicken, curry cut
- 4–6 green chillies
- 1 bunch fresh coriander
- ½ cup curd (yogurt)
- 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
- Salt to taste

### For the Chicken
- 2 tbsp oil
- 2 large onions, thinly sliced
- 2 cardamom pods
- 4 cloves
- 1 small cinnamon stick
- 2 bay leaves
- ½ tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- 1 tsp cumin powder
- ½ tsp garam masala

### For the Rice
- 2 cups basmati rice, soaked 30 minutes
- Water, as needed
- A small bunch of coriander (cilantro)
- 1 tsp ginger garlic paste
- 2 cardamom pods
- 1 blade mace (javitri)
- 2–3 marathi mokku (dried kapok buds)
- Salt to taste

---

## Method

### 1. Marinate the Chicken

1. Blend green chillies, fresh coriander, curd, and salt into a smooth paste.
2. Mix in ginger garlic paste.
3. Coat the chicken well with the marinade and set aside for at least **1 hour** (overnight in the fridge is even better).

### 2. Cook the Chicken

1. Heat oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat.
2. Add cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaves. Let them sizzle for 30 seconds.
3. Add sliced onions and fry until deep golden brown.
4. Add turmeric, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Stir for 30 seconds.
5. Add the marinated chicken and cook on high heat for 5 minutes, stirring well.
6. Reduce heat, cover, and cook until the chicken is nearly done and the gravy has reduced to a thick, clinging consistency — about 15–20 minutes.
7. Finish with garam masala, stir, and take off the heat.

### 3. Parboil the Rice

1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
2. Add coriander, ginger garlic paste, cardamom, mace (javitri), marathi mokku, and salt.
3. Add the soaked and drained rice.
4. Cook until the rice is **80% done** — it should still have a slight bite in the centre.
5. Drain immediately and set aside.

### 4. Dum (Slow Cook)

1. Layer the parboiled rice evenly over the chicken in the pot.
2. Cover tightly with a lid (seal with dough or foil if you have it).
3. Cook on the lowest heat for **10 minutes**.
4. Turn off the heat and let it rest, undisturbed, for **15 minutes**.
5. Gently mix from the bottom before serving.

---

## Tips

- **Marathi mokku** (dried kapok flower buds) are a signature spice in Andhra-style biryanis and pulaos — don't skip them if you can find them.
- The green chilli paste quantity can be adjusted to heat preference, but this dish is meant to be bold and spicy.
- Sealing the pot with dough during dum gives a better steam lock and richer flavour.
- Serve with raita, sliced onions, and a wedge of lime.
