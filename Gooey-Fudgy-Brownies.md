# Gooey Fudgy Brownies

Rich, ultra-fudgy brownies with glossy crackly tops, deep chocolate flavor, and a gooey center. Perfect served warm with vanilla ice cream.

## Yield
One 9×13-inch pan (or extra-thick brownies in an 8×8-inch pan)

## Ingredients

### Brown Butter Chocolate Base
- 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter
- 1 cup (90g) Dutch-process cocoa powder
- 4 oz (113g) dark or semisweet chocolate (60% cacao), finely chopped
- 1 tsp instant espresso powder  
  *(or 1 Tbsp instant coffee)*

### Batter
- 1½ cups (300g) granulated sugar
- ½ cup (100g) brown sugar
- 1 Tbsp vanilla extract
- 4 large eggs
- 1 cup (120g) all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp coarse kosher salt

### Optional Mix-Ins
- 1 cup chopped chocolate or chocolate chips
- 1–2 cups chopped walnuts, pecans, dried cherries, etc.

---

# Instructions

### 1. Prep the Pan
Preheat oven to **350°F (175°C)**.

Line a 9×13-inch baking pan with parchment paper or foil, leaving overhang on the sides for easy removal.

---

### 2. Prepare the Chocolate Mixture
In a large heatproof bowl, combine:
- cocoa powder
- chopped chocolate
- espresso powder

Set aside.

---

### 3. Brown the Butter
In a light-colored saucepan over medium-low heat, melt the butter.

As it foams and sputters, stir frequently and scrape the bottom of the pan every 10–15 seconds. After several minutes, the milk solids will begin turning golden brown.

Continue cooking until:
- the bubbling quiets,
- the milk solids become deep brown,
- and the butter smells nutty and toasted.

Immediately remove from heat to prevent burning.

---

### 4. Bloom the Cocoa
Pour the hot brown butter directly over the cocoa/chocolate mixture.

Stir until completely smooth and glossy. Let cool slightly.

---

### 5. Whip the Eggs and Sugar
Using a stand mixer or hand mixer, beat together:
- granulated sugar
- brown sugar
- vanilla
- eggs

Mix on medium-high speed until thick, fluffy, and lighter in color.

- **4–5 minutes** → denser, richer, gooier brownies
- **8–10 minutes** → slightly taller brownies with more structure

---

### 6. Combine Wet Ingredients
Reduce mixer speed to low.

Slowly pour the chocolate-butter mixture into the whipped egg mixture and mix until fully combined.

---

### 7. Fold in Dry Ingredients
Using a spatula, gently fold in:
- flour
- salt

Mix just until incorporated.

Fold in any optional mix-ins if using. Batter will be very thick.

---

### 8. Fill the Pan
Spread batter evenly into prepared pan.

---

### 9. Bake
Bake for **about 25 minutes**, or until:
- the top is glossy,
- edges are set,
- and the center is no longer jiggly.

Do **not** overbake — the brownies will continue setting as they cool.

---

### 10. Cool and Serve
Cool at least **30 minutes** before lifting from the pan and slicing.

Serve warm or room temperature.

Excellent with vanilla ice cream.

---

# Notes & Tips

- **Whipped eggs + white sugar** create the shiny, crackly brownie top.
- **Brown butter** adds depth and removes excess moisture for richer flavor.
- If skipping brown butter:
  - Melt butter together with chopped chocolate,
  - then pour over cocoa and espresso powder.
- For **extra-thick brownies**, bake in an 8×8-inch pan and increase bake time slightly.
- Slightly underbaking gives the fudgiest texture.
